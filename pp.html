Tourism

Royal Palace

A symbol of Cambodia’s monarchy and spiritual pride, the Royal Palace impresses with its golden spires, traditional Khmer architecture, and serene gardens. Inside, the Silver Pagoda dazzles with its floor of silver tiles and priceless Buddha statues.

Walking Street

Stroll along Phnom Penh’s lively riverside promenade, where local life meets international charm. With scenic views of the Tonlé Sap River, street food vendors, and vibrant night vibes, it’s the perfect place to unwind, people-watch, or catch the sunset.

National Museum of Cambodia

Home to the world’s largest collection of Khmer art, this elegant terracotta museum showcases ancient sculptures, artifacts, and religious relics. A peaceful garden courtyard complements your cultural journey through Cambodia’s rich artistic and spiritual heritage.

Tuol Sleng Genocide Museum (S21)

A powerful and sobering memorial, Tuol Sleng was once a high school turned prison during the Khmer Rouge regime. Today, it stands as a haunting reminder of Cambodia’s tragic past, offering visitors an emotional journey through history with original photographs, survivor stories, and preserved prison cells.

Phsar Thmey (Central Market)

An iconic art-deco landmark, Phsar Thmey bustles with energy, offering everything from souvenirs and clothing to electronics and street food. Its distinctive yellow dome and maze-like stalls make it a must-visit for shoppers and photographers alike.
