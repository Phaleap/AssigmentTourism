Welcome to MondulKiri

Why Mondul Kiri?

Because it’s Cambodia’s wild soul — untouched, unfiltered, and unforgettable. Mondul Kiri isn’t just another province; it’s an experience. Nestled in the eastern highlands, this lush, elevated region is home to waterfalls that roar like giants, elephants that roam with wisdom, and hill tribes that preserve centuries of tradition. Here, the air is cooler, the forests deeper, and the pace of life slower — a perfect contrast to the heat and bustle of the cities. From the majestic Bou Sra Waterfall to the peaceful Elephant Valley Project, every corner of Mondul Kiri offers something that whispers, “Stay a little longer.” You don’t just visit Mondul Kiri — you reconnect with nature, with culture, and with yourself.

Bou Sra Waterfall & Leng Khin Waterfall

Hidden deep in Mondul Kiri’s misty highlands, Bou Sra Waterfall crashes down with a force that echoes through the forest like thunder. It's not just a waterfall — it’s a two-tiered natural giant, wrapped in lush greenery and tribal legends. Standing at its edge, you feel small — in the best way. The powerful flow, especially during rainy season, feels alive, breathing energy into everything around it. Whether you’re taking photos, feeling the cool mist on your face, or listening to the stories of the Bunong people, Bou Sra doesn’t just impress — it humbles you.

While Bou Sra roars, Leng Khin whispers. Tucked away in the serene embrace of the forest, Leng Khin Waterfall is a peaceful escape, where time seems to slow and nature speaks gently. Here, the water flows over smooth rocks, creating shallow pools perfect for a refreshing dip. Butterflies dance through the air, birds call from the trees, and the only soundtrack is the soft rush of water. It’s the kind of place that invites you to sit, breathe, and let the wild calm your soul — a true hidden gem waiting for curious travelers.

Phnong Ethnic Village

The Phnong people, also known as the Bunong, are an indigenous group native to Mondulkiri province. Deeply connected to nature, they live in stilted houses, speak their own language, and practice animism—believing that spirits dwell in forests, rivers, and animals. Elephants hold a special place in their culture, often seen not just as working animals but as sacred companions. Visitors can explore Phnong villages, learn about their traditional crafts and herbal medicine, and experience a lifestyle that has remained largely untouched by modern influence. The Phnong community offers a unique cultural window into Cambodia’s rich indigenous heritage.

Elephant Valley Project

Step into the heart of Mondulkiri’s forest and meet Cambodia’s rescued elephants like never before. The Elephant Valley Project isn’t a zoo—it’s a sanctuary where elephants roam freely, play in rivers, and enjoy the jungle in peace. No rides, no tricks—just elephants being elephants in their natural world.

Experience a deeper bond by walking alongside these majestic creatures. As you trek through the hills with the elephants, you’ll witness how they interact, forage, and even form friendships. The focus here is on respect and rehabilitation, making it a truly ethical and unforgettable experience.

Support wildlife conservation while exploring breathtaking scenery. Every visit contributes to local communities and forest protection. Learn about elephant behavior, care, and the incredible efforts being made to protect them—all while enjoying the tranquil beauty of Mondulkiri’s untouched jungle.
